What does Covisearch do?
Covisearch is a search engine, sort of like Google, but specially for Covid resources. It searches multiple Covid help and resource websites on the internet and shows combined results, sorted by relevance. Typically, this means that the most recently verified resources across multiple websites appear on Covisearch.
There are already many helpful websites and help groups for Covid resources. How does Covisearch help?
There is lot of information online, but it is scattered across many websites. Then there is the problem of duplicate information on multiple websites. This makes the jobs of volunteers tedious at best. We hope to fix this by showing all relevant data in one place so volunteers and people can save crucial time.
Who are you?
We’re just a bunch of software developers with a regular day job, combined with the zeal to do something for the people in this hour of need.
How does it work?
Like a search engine. When you search for a resource, say Oxygen, in a city, say Mumbai, our service automatically visits all the Covid help websites in our list, gathers all the information together, removes duplicates, sorts the list by relevance (like most recently verified entries first), and shows it to you. It also saves those entries on our servers. Once searched for the first time, we refresh entries for that filter (Eg: Oxygen in Mumbai) periodically to keep the data updated on our end. So the next time you search for the same filter, you get information straight from our servers.
How often do you refresh the entries?
Every few hours.
Which websites do you search on?
We search on all the significant pan-india websites which came up during the second wave of Covid in India. For a complete list, click here.
Aren’t you stealing data of other websites and showing it as your own?
We don’t lay any claim over the data we show. Our goal is only to make it easier for people to reach these scattered resources quicker. We deeply appreciate the work Covid help and resource websites are doing, and we salute the volunteers who tirelessly verify resources every day. That is why we provide links to the original sources on the resource information shown on our website. These links take you to the websites where we found the data.
Do you partner with any websites for the data?
We don’t have direct partnership with any of these websites yet. However, some groups have been kind enough to grant us access to use their backend APIs to directly fetch their data. Also, we’re open to any partnerships which may benefit everyone. See the ‘How do I reach out to you?’ to contact us.
Do you have your own volunteers for verification or help with finding resources?
No. We’re not a group with our own volunteers who can help find and verify resources. We are only trying to ease the task of finding resources easy for other volunteer groups and families.
Can I get access to your data? Is there an API?
We are glad to share our searched data with everyone. We do have an API which we use from our website too. The API documentation is still in progress. However, feel free to reach out to us if you need access to our API. See the next question.
